🌐 Geopolitical Impact — Active Themes Today
🛢️ US–Iran peace framework & the oil collapse — the dominant market driver
A 14-point US–Iran memorandum of understanding (reached 14–15 Jun, signing in Switzerland) extends the ceasefire ~60 days, reopens the Strait of Hormuz toll-free and lifts the US naval blockade. Crucially, Iran can sell crude immediately with sanctions relief. Brent fell ~5% to below $80 — its lowest since early March — and WTI to ~$76, after oil had spiked >50% during the late-Feb war. Analysts caution it could take 3–6 months to fully normalise flows and the deal is a framework, not a final treaty. For oil-importing India this is a clean macro positive (current account, inflation, INR).
Beneficiaries
▲ Indian OMCs / importers BPCL, HPCL, IOC — lower crude, better marketing margins
▲ Airlines INDIGO, US carriers DAL/UAL/AAL — jet fuel is 20–30% of costs
▲ Crude-input consumers ASIANPAINT (paints), MRF/Apollo (tyres); US consumer cyclicals
Victims
▼ Oil producers XOM, CVX, OXY, ONGC, OIL — weaker realisations
▼ Oilfield services / tankers SLB; FRO, STNG as Hormuz war-premium fades
▼ Gold / safe-havens Risk-off unwind pressures gold after its war-time run
Sources
NBC News  ·  CNBC  ·  Al Jazeera
🏦 Hawkish Fed under new chair Kevin Warsh
At its first meeting chaired by Kevin Warsh (17 Jun) the Fed held rates but signalled at least one possible hike later in 2026 to tamp inflation; Treasury yields surged and stocks sold off that day before rebounding on the Iran deal. A stronger-than-expected May jobs report (172k vs ~80k expected, partly World-Cup-related hiring) reinforced the hawkish tilt. Higher US yields are a mild headwind for emerging-market flows, including India.
